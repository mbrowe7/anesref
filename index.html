Malignant Hyperthermia
Volatile / Sux trigger — RyR1 mutation
›
⚠️ Earliest signs: unexplained ↑EtCO₂ + tachycardia. Hyperthermia is LATE.
1
Call for help. Activate MH protocol. Call MHAUS: 1-800-644-9737
2
Stop trigger. D/C all volatile agents and succinylcholine immediately.
3
Flush circuit with 100% O₂ at ≥10 L/min high flow. Hyperventilate ×2–3 normal MV.
4
Dantrolene 2.5 mg/kg IV bolus — repeat q5–10 min. Initial max: 10 mg/kg. Mix each vial with 60 mL sterile water.
5
Cool actively: ice to axilla/groin, cold IV fluids, cold gastric/bladder lavage. Target core <38.5°C.
6
Treat complications:
Hyperkalemia → bicarb + insulin/D50 Acidosis → bicarb Arrhythmia → amiodarone (NOT CCBs) DIC → FFP/PLT
7
ICU admission. Continue dantrolene 1 mg/kg q6h × 24–48h. Monitor CK, K⁺, urine output (target >1 mL/kg/hr).
💊 Dantrolene math: 2.5 mg/kg × 70 kg = 175 mg = ~6 vials (each vial = 20 mg + 3g mannitol). Have 36 vials (720 mg) on hand.

Anaphylaxis
NMBAs are #1 cause intraop (>50%)
›
Under GA: skin signs may be ABSENT. Look for ↓BP + bronchospasm + ↑airway pressures.
1
Stop the trigger. Call for help. Note time.
2
Epinephrine (FIRST LINE — do not delay):
10–100 mcg IV bolus — repeat q1–5 min
If refractory: 0.05–0.3 mcg/kg/min infusion
3
IV fluids. Bolus 1–2 L crystalloid rapidly for distributive shock.
4
100% O₂. Secure/confirm airway. Treat bronchospasm with salbutamol nebs, ketamine, or heliox.
5
Adjuncts (after epi + fluids):
Diphenhydramine 50 mg IVRanitidine 50 mg IVHydrocortisone 200 mg IV
6
Draw serum tryptase ASAP — peaks at 30–90 min. Repeat at 2h and 24h.
7
ICU observation × 24h (biphasic reaction risk). Allergy follow-up at 4–6 weeks.
💉
LAST
Local Anesthetic Systemic Toxicity
›
Signs: CNS excitation → seizures → cardiovascular collapse. Bupivacaine is most cardiotoxic.
1
Stop injection immediately. Call for help. Get Lipid Rescue Kit.
2
Airway: 100% O₂, secure airway, prevent hypoxia and acidosis (worsen cardiotoxicity).
3
Seizures: Benzodiazepines first. Midazolam 1–2 mg IV or lorazepam 1–2 mg IV. Avoid propofol if hemodynamically unstable.
4
Intralipid 20% protocol:
Bolus: 1.5 mL/kg IV over 1 min
Infusion: 0.25 mL/kg/min
Repeat bolus ×2 if no response q5 min
Max total: 10 mL/kg
5
Cardiac arrest: CPR + low-dose epi <1 mcg/kg. Avoid vasopressin, CCBs, β-blockers, and high-dose epi.
6
Refractory arrest: Cardiopulmonary bypass. Alert cardiac surgery early.
✓ ACMT/ASRA Lipid Rescue: www.lipidrescue.org — report all cases
🫁
CICO
Can't Intubate, Can't Oxygenate
›
⚠️ Patients die from HYPOXIA — not inability to intubate. If SpO₂ dropping: ACT NOW.
1
CALL FOR HELP immediately if not already done. State "CICO emergency."
2
Max oxygenation attempts:
OPA/NPA + 2-person maskReposition (sniff/ramp)Attempt different SGAHigh-flow nasal O₂
3
Limit attempts: Max 3 per technique. If 2 DL attempts fail → video laryngoscopy or fiberoptic. Different blade, different person each time.
4
If SpO₂ failing → Emergency surgical airway NOW. Do not delay for one more intubation attempt.
5
Scalpel-Bougie Cricothyrotomy:
① Vertical midline skin incision
② Palpate CTM → horizontal stab incision
③ Rotate scalpel 180° → extend incision
④ Finger in trachea → pass bougie caudally
⑤ Railroad 6.0 cuffed ETT over bougie
⑥ Confirm: EtCO₂ + chest rise
💡 If you're even thinking about a cric kit — call for it NOW. Better to have it and not need it.

Transfusion Reaction
Stop blood — identify reaction type
›
STOP THE TRANSFUSION IMMEDIATELY. Notify attending, surgeon, and blood bank.
Reaction types:
Acute hemolytic (ABO incompatibility)
Tachycardia, ↓BP, brown urine, diffuse oozing under GA. Rx: stop blood, alkalinize urine (bicarb + mannitol + furosemide), supportive care for DIC.
Anaphylactic (IgA deficiency)
Immediate shock + bronchospasm. Rx: epinephrine, fluids, antihistamines. Future: washed blood products.
TRALI
4–6h post-transfusion. Bilateral pulmonary edema, ↓SpO₂, ↓BP, PCWP normal. Rx: supportive (O₂, vent) — NO diuretics (not volume overload).
Febrile non-hemolytic
Benign. Tylenol, slow rate. Future: leukoreduced blood.
😤
Laryngospasm
Reflex vocal cord closure
›
1
Remove stimulus. Call for help.
2
Positive pressure O₂ via tight-fitting mask. Jaw thrust + CPAP 20–30 cmH₂O.
3
Larson's maneuver: firm pressure in the "laryngospasm notch" (posterior to ear lobe, anterior to mastoid).
4
If not breaking: Propofol 0.5–1 mg/kg IV to deepen anesthesia.
5
If SpO₂ dropping and not breaking: Succinylcholine 0.1–0.5 mg/kg IV or 4 mg/kg IM (with atropine in peds).

ASA Monitoring Standards
Required for every anesthetic
›
Oxygenation
SpO₂ + FiO₂ analyzer with low O₂ alarm
Ventilation
Capnography + disconnect alarm if mechanically ventilated
Circulation
EKG + NIBP q5min + one continuous (SpO₂ waveform, a-line, or pulse)
Temperature
If clinically significant ΔT anticipated
Continual = repeated regularly (q5 min BP). Continuous = uninterrupted (EKG display, qualified personnel presence).
